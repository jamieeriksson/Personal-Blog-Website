I grew up in Dallas, TX where I also attended the University of Texas at Dallas to pursue a BS in Mechanical Engineering. Since then I have played 5 years of college ultimate, and 4 years of club ultimate where I have attended club Nationals twice.

The Pains of Burnout

Been dealing with a little bit of burnout but have been doing my best to take care of myself so that I can get back into things strongly again. I’ve been working on some throwing form adjustments. I tweaked my forehand grip and have been working on my arm mechanics to be able to start throwing with my elbow away from my body/hips. The motions are starting to feel more comfortable so I’m excited to see how it progresses. I was also working on backhand throws this evening with a focus on the wind-up and pull through and pulling along a straight line. My backhands felt really strong, so that’s a win.

I kind of touched on this in a story from this past weekend, but I’ve started doing front squats for the first time. The first photo of them was really my first time doing them, and I let my ego push the weight at 105lbs which resulted in unideal form. The second photo was my “fix” by going back down to 85lbs so that I could really focus on the quality of the movement and implement some tips Mike gave me @haddocksportperformance. It’s hard swallowing my pride sometimes. It’s easy for me to get caught on this identity of feeling like I need to “be strong” and show/exemplify that through heavier weights. I had to take a moment and remind myself that it’s okay to be okay with starting somewhere that I maybe wish I didn’t have to start. But even more so, I realized that getting to start here is exciting, it’s amazing. It means that my ceiling is still above me and that I have the opportunity to improve, to get better, and to push myself. It means that there is still some untapped potential that I get to build in myself. I am still able to be better. And that makes me feel pretty darned lucky. #HSPathlete
